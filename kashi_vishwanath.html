Temple Overview

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, located in the spiritual capital of Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, is the twelfth and most celebrated among the sacred Jyotirlingas. Known as the "Golden Temple" for its magnificent golden spire, this ancient shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva as Vishwanath (Lord of the Universe) attracts over 45,000 pilgrims daily. Built by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar in 1780, the temple stands majestically near the sacred Ganga, with the revolutionary Kashi Vishwanath Corridor (inaugurated in 2021) connecting devotees directly from the temple to the holy river.

Historical Timeline

Ancient Origins (Puranic Era)

According to ancient scriptures, Lord Shiva established himself as Vishwanath (Lord of the Universe) in Kashi (Varanasi), making it the eternal city where devotees attain moksha. The original temple, called Adi Vishveshwar, existed for millennia as the primary Jyotirlinga shrine.

1194 CE - First Destruction

The original Adi Vishveshwar Temple was demolished by Mohammad of Ghor during his invasion. Despite repeated destructions over centuries, Hindu devotees continued worship and reconstruction efforts, demonstrating unwavering faith.

1585-1669 CE

Man Singh I and Todar Mal rebuilt the temple under Emperor Akbar's reign. However, in 1669, Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb ordered its demolition, and the Gyanvapi Mosque was constructed on the site in 1678, though Hindu pilgrims continued visiting.

1780 CE - Present Temple

Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore constructed the current magnificent temple on an adjacent site. In 1785, Mohammad Ibrahim Khan built the Naubatkhana (drum house). In 1839, Maharaja Ranjit Singh donated gold for covering two of the temple's domes.

1983-2021 Modern Era

Government of Uttar Pradesh took over temple management in 1983, establishing a trust with the former Kashi Naresh as president. The historic Kumbhabhishekham (consecration) was performed in 2018 after 239 years.

2021-2025 Revolutionary Phase

PM Modi inaugurated the Kashi Vishwanath Dham Corridor in December 2021, transforming the temple experience. In 2022, the sanctum sanctorum was gold-plated with 60 kg gold. Over 10 crore visitors have experienced the new world-class facilities.

Vishwanath - Lord of the Universe

Kashi Vishwanath represents Lord Shiva's supreme form as the ruler of all cosmic creation. According to Hindu scriptures, dying in Kashi (Varanasi) grants instant moksha (liberation) as Lord Shiva himself whispers the Taraka Mantra in the ear of the departing soul, freeing them from rebirth cycles.
