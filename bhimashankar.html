Temple Overview

The Bhimashankar Temple, located in the picturesque Sahyadri Hills of Maharashtra, is the sixth among the 12 revered Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva. Situated 125 km from Pune in the village of Bhorgiri, this 13th-century temple is uniquely positioned within a declared Wildlife Sanctuary, making it a rare combination of spiritual devotion and natural conservation. The temple marks the origin of the sacred Bhima River and attracts hundreds of thousands of devotees and nature enthusiasts annually.

Historical Timeline

Ancient Legend (Puranic Era)

According to Shiva Purana, the demon Bhimasur (son of Kumbhakarna) performed severe penance after his father's death. Lord Brahma granted him immense power, which he misused to terrorize the world. Lord Shiva manifested as Bhimashankar to defeat him, establishing the Jyotirlinga.

13th Century CE

The original temple structure was built during this period, showcasing early Nagara architectural style. The temple was strategically positioned at the source of the Bhima River, which flows east across the Deccan plateau.

17th-18th Century (Maratha Era)

Significant patronage under Maratha rulers including Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, who made generous endowments. Nana Phadnavis constructed the Sabha Mandapa in the 18th century. Chimaji Appa offered a large bell from Vasai Fort after defeating the Portuguese.

1985 CE

Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary was officially notified under the Wildlife Protection Act by Maharashtra Government, making it unique among Jyotirlingas to be located within a protected biodiversity area.

Modern Era (1990-Present)

Development of infrastructure including better road connectivity from Pune, online booking systems, and eco-tourism facilities. The temple now balances religious significance with wildlife conservation, attracting both devotees and nature lovers.

Architectural Marvel

Nagara Style Features

  • Composite Structure: Blend of 13th-century original and 18th-century additions
  • Shikhara Design: Elegant spire built by Nana Phadnavis with intricate stone work
  • Sabha Mandapa: Spacious assembly hall added during Peshwa period
  • Stone Carvings: Excellent Vishwakarma sculptural work depicting various deities
  • Bell Collection: Historic bells including one gifted by Chimaji Appa from Vasai Fort
Bhima River Source

The temple is located at the origin of river Bhima, which flows eastward through Maharashtra and Karnataka, eventually joining the Krishna River near Raichur.

Lord of the Sahyadris

Bhimashankar represents Lord Shiva's victory over evil forces and his role as protector of nature. The temple's location within a Wildlife Sanctuary symbolizes the harmony between spiritual devotion and environmental conservation, making it a unique pilgrimage destination.
