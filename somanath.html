Somnath Temple

The First and Eternal Jyotirlinga of Lord Shiva

Somnath - "The Protector of Moon God" - stands as the first among the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas, a symbol of eternal faith and India's indomitable spirit of reconstruction.

Temple Overview

The Somnath Temple, also known as Deo Patan, is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites in India, receiving millions of devotees annually. Located on the western coast of Gujarat at Prabhas Patan, Veraval, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva as the first Jyotirlinga, embodying infinite light and divine energy.

Historical Timeline

Ancient Era

Original temple believed to be built by Moon God Chandra in gold, later rebuilt by Ravana in silver, Krishna in wood, and King Bhimdev in stone according to legends.

7th-10th Century

Temple reached zenith under various Hindu dynasties including Maitraka and Chalukya rulers. Became renowned across the ancient world for its wealth and grandeur.

1025-1706 CE

Series of invasions and destructions by Mahmud of Ghazni (1025), Alauddin Khalji (1299), Ahmad Shah I (1395), and Aurangzeb (1706). Each time rebuilt by devoted Hindu rulers.

1947-1951

Modern reconstruction initiated by Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel after independence. Foundation laid on 8 May 1950, completed under K.M. Munshi's Somnath Trust. Consecrated by President Rajendra Prasad on 11 May 1951.

Symbol of Resilience

Destroyed 17 times, rebuilt each time with unwavering faith
