Temple Overview

The Trimbakeshwar Temple, located in Trimbak village near Nashik, Maharashtra, is the tenth among the 12 sacred Jyotirlingas and holds the unique distinction of housing a three-faced lingam representing the Tridev - Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva. Situated at the foothills of Brahmagiri Hills where the sacred Godavari River originates, this 18th-century temple built by Peshwa Balaji Bajirao (1755-1786) attracts millions of devotees annually. The temple is renowned for the Kushavarta Tirtha, a sacred pond located 400m from the temple, believed to be where Ganga reemerges after disappearing at Brahmagiri Hills.

Historical Timeline

Ancient Legend (Puranic Era)

According to Shiva Purana, Sage Gautama performed penance at Brahmagiri Hills to bring Ganga to earth. Lord Shiva was pleased and manifested the Godavari River, establishing himself as Trimbakeshwar (three-faced form representing Tridev) at this sacred location.

Ancient Temple Period

An ancient temple existed at this site for centuries before the current structure. Historical records indicate continuous worship and pilgrimage to this Jyotirlinga from ancient times, with mentions in various Puranas and sacred texts.

1750 CE - Kushavarta Construction

The sacred Kushavarta Tirtha (holy pond) was constructed in 1750, creating a 21-foot deep reservoir believed to be where Ganga reemerges after disappearing at Brahmagiri Hills. This enhanced the temple's spiritual significance significantly.

1755-1786 CE - Present Temple

Peshwa Balaji Bajirao (Nanasaheb Peshwa) constructed the current magnificent temple using black stone in Hemadpanthi architectural style. The temple features four directional gates representing East (beginning), West (maturity), South (completion), and North (revelation).

1954 CE - Trust Formation

Shri Trimbakeshwar Sansthan was registered under Public Trust Registration Act in 1954, formalizing temple administration. The trust manages daily three-time pooja, festivals, and all religious activities throughout the year.

Modern Era (2000-Present)

Development of modern facilities including Shivprasad Bhaktaniwas (24 rooms), online booking systems, VIP darshan passes, and digital donation platforms. Introduction of live online pujas accessible globally via video conferencing.

The temple's location at Godavari's origin makes it doubly sacred, as both river worship and Shiva worship converge here in perfect spiritual harmony.
