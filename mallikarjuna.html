Temple Overview

Sri Bhramaramba Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple at Srisailam is one of India's most sacred pilgrimage sites, uniquely combining both Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peetha significance. Located atop the Nallamala Hills overlooking the Krishna River, this ancient shrine attracts millions of devotees seeking the divine blessings of Lord Mallikarjuna and Goddess Bhramaramba.

Built:

Ancient temple with major reconstructions in 7th century, 15th century Vijayanagara period, and recent renovations

Architecture:

Magnificent Dravidian style with four golden gopurams, mandapams, and intricate stone carvings

Location:

Srisailam, Kurnool District, Andhra Pradesh - atop Nallamala Hills by Krishna River

Deities:

Lord Mallikarjuna (2nd Jyotirlinga) & Goddess Bhramaramba (6th Shakti Peetha)

Significance:

Unique temple combining both Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peetha - "Kailash of South"

Visitors:

Over 4 million pilgrims annually, especially during Shivaratri and Navaratri festivals

Historical Timeline

Ancient Era

According to Skanda Purana, when Kartikeya was upset about Ganesha's marriage, Lord Shiva and Parvati came to console him at this place, which became sacred as Srisailam - the abode of Shri (prosperity).

7th Century CE

Main shrine and Jyotirlinga established. The self-manifested lingam worshipped with jasmine flowers (Mallika in Telugu) gave the deity the name Mallikarjuna.

12th-14th Century

Temple patronized by Kakatiya dynasty and later by Vijayanagara Empire. Significant expansions and architectural developments during this period.

15th-16th Century

Major reconstruction under Sri Krishnadevaraya of Vijayanagara Empire. Eastern Rajagopuram and many mandapams built during this golden period.

17th Century

Chatrapati Shivaji constructed one of the gopurams. The temple received patronage from various rulers including the Marathas and later the Nizams of Hyderabad.

Modern Era

Under Andhra Pradesh government, extensive renovations and facilities development. Srisaila Devasthanam established for better temple administration and pilgrim services.

Architectural Marvel

Dravidian Style Features

  • Four Gopurams: Magnificent gateway towers with intricate carvings
  • Golden Gopuram: One tower completely covered in gold plating
  • Mukha Mandapa: Grand hall from Vijayanagara period with 42 pillars
  • Garbhagriha: 60 sq feet sanctum with self-manifested Jyotirlinga
  • Nandi Mandapa: Central hall with magnificent Nandi statue
  • Mirror Hall: Beautiful hall with Nataraja images

Nandi Mandapa

Central hall with imposing Nandi statue facing the main shrine, supported by ornate pillars

Spiritual Significance

Divine Legend & Importance

According to the sacred Skanda Purana, when Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati decided to find suitable brides for their sons, Ganesha was married to Riddhi and Siddhi. Upon returning, Kartikeya was upset and went to Mount Krauncha. When Shiva and Parvati came to console him, the divine place where they stayed became known as Srisailam - the abode of prosperity.

Unique Dual Significance
  • 2nd Jyotirlinga: Sacred pillar of divine light representing Lord Shiva
  • 6th Shakti Peetha: Where Goddess Sati's upper lip fell, manifesting as Bhramaramba
  • Divine Union: Only place where Shiva and Shakti reside together in temple complex
  • Kailash of South: Considered equivalent to Mount Kailash for southern devotees
