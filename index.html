Sacred Jyotirlingas

First Jyotirlinga

Somnath

Gujarat, India
The Eternal Shrine

The first among the 12 Jyotirlingas, destroyed and rebuilt multiple times, symbolizing the eternal nature of faith and devotion.

Eternal Shrine Arabian Sea Trust Temple
Second Jyotirlinga

Mallikarjuna

Andhra Pradesh, India
Mountain of Enlightenment

Perched atop Srisailam hills, this ancient temple combines Shiva and Shakti worship in a serene mountain setting.

Hill Temple Shakti Peeth Forest Setting


Third Jyotirlinga

Mahakaleshwar

Madhya Pradesh, India
Lord of Time

The only Jyotirlinga facing south (Dakshinmukhi), famous for its divine Bhasma Aarti performed with sacred ash from the cremation grounds.

Dakshinmukhi Bhasma Aarti Time Lord
Fourth Jyotirlinga

Omkareshwar

Madhya Pradesh, India
Sacred Om Symbol

Located on the Om-shaped Mandhata Island in the Narmada River, representing the primordial sound of creation.

Om Island Narmada River Sacred Sound
Fifth Jyotirlinga

Kedarnath

Uttarakhand, India
Himalayan Heights

Situated at 3,583 meters in the Himalayas, accessible only 6 months a year, representing ultimate spiritual challenges and rewards.

Himalayan High Altitude Char Dham
Sixth Jyotirlinga

Bhimashankar

Maharashtra, India
Forest Sanctuary

Nestled in the dense forests of the Western Ghats, surrounded by wildlife sanctuary and pristine nature.

Wildlife Sanctuary Western Ghats Forest Temple
Seventh Jyotirlinga

Kashi Vishwanath

Uttar Pradesh, India
Golden Temple

The most famous Jyotirlinga in the spiritual capital Varanasi, with gold-plated spires and the divine Kashi Vishwanath Corridor.

Golden Spires Holy Varanasi Ganga Ghats
Eighth Jyotirlinga

Vaidyanath

Jharkhand, India
Divine Physician

Known as Baba Baidyanath Dham, both Jyotirlinga and Shakti Peeth, famous for miraculous healing powers and Kanwar Yatra.

Healing Powers Shakti Peeth Kanwar Yatra

Gujarat, India
Lord of Serpents

Located on the Saurashtra coast near Dwarka, featuring a 25-meter Shiva statue and protection from all poisons and fears.

Trimbakeshwar

Unique three-faced lingam representing Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva at the origin of sacred Godavari River.

Three-faced Lingam River Origin Trinity Power

Rameshwaram

Tamil Nadu, India
Sacred Island

Rama's Temple Char Dham Sacred Island

Final Jyotirlinga UNESCO Proximity Divine Compassion

About Jyotirlingas

The 12 Jyotirlingas are the most sacred shrines of Lord Shiva, representing his infinite nature as columns of light (Jyoti) that pierce through the earth and heavens. Each Jyotirlinga is believed to be a Swayambhu (self-existent) manifestation where Lord Shiva appeared as a blinding pillar of light.

According to ancient scriptures, visiting all 12 Jyotirlingas is considered the ultimate pilgrimage for devotees of Lord Shiva, bringing liberation from the cycle of birth and death, and granting divine blessings for spiritual enlightenment.

Spiritual Peace

Experience divine tranquility and inner peace through sacred darshan and prayers.

Divine Protection

Receive Lord Shiva's divine protection from negative influences and life obstacles.

Spiritual Liberation

Attain moksha (liberation) and freedom from the endless cycle of birth and death.

Divine Blessings

Receive abundant blessings for health, prosperity, and family harmony.
